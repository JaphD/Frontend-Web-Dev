1984

Set in a totalitarian society under constant surveillance, "1984" depicts a dystopian world where individuality is suppressed and truth is manipulated by the ruling Party. George Orwell's novel explores themes of oppression, propaganda, and the erosion of personal freedoms. Through the protagonist Winston Smith's rebellion against the oppressive regime and his illicit love affair with Julia, Orwell paints a chilling portrait of a future where dissent is punishable by torture and the rewriting of history is commonplace. "1984" remains a powerful warning against authoritarianism and a timeless exploration of the consequences of unchecked state power on human society.
